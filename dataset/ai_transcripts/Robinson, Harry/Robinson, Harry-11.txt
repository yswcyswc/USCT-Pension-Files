                            GENERAL AFFIDAVIT.

State of  South Carolina , County of  Colleton                           , SS:
    In the matter of  Flora Robinson Widow of Harry Robinson
late Private in Co G. 34 Regt. U.S.C.I.
On this  28      day of  March         A. D. one thousand eight hundred and
ninety- two, personally appeared before me  C. G. Henderson Master
in Equity                    within and for the County and State
aforesaid, duly authorized to administer oaths,  Lucius Robinson
aged  66     years, a resident of  ___              County of
Colleton        and State of  South Carolina     well known
to me to be reputable and entitled to credit, and who, being duly sworn, declared in relation to
aforesaid case as follows:
that he was a private in Co G. 34 U.S.C.I. with
Harry Robinson, that said Harry Robinson was
discharged for acute rheumatism by Dr. Hansam
Regimental Surgeon about two months before he was
taken, from which he never recovered, and died
from same in January 1875. Deponent being present
at his death — who was buried on Cypress plantation
in Colleton County — Deponent now draws
a pension himself, and was in no way related
to Harry Robinson, although bearing the same
surname.


H's Post-Office address is  Green Pond Colleton Co. S.C.
He further declare that  he has  no interest in said case and  is   not concerned
in its prosecution.

James F. Mack                            Lucius  his
                                                  X Robinson
