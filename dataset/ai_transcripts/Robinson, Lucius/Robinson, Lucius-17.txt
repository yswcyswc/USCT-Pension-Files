                        NOTES.
The act of June 27, 1890, requires, in case of a soldier:
  (1) An honorable discharge (but the certificate need not be filed unless called for).
  (2) A minimum service of ninety days.
  (3) A mental or physical disability of a permanent character not due to vicious habits.  (It need not have originated in
the service.)
  (4) The rates under the act are graded from $6 to $12, proportioned to the degree of inability to earn a support, and are not
affected by the rank held.
  (5) A pensioner under prior laws may apply under this one, or a pensioner under this one may apply under other laws, but
he cannot draw more than one pension for the same period.
